 I. United Leninism → United Asian Revolutionary Bolshevik Party
Self-Name: United Bolsheviks / United Bolshevik Party / Party
Armed revolution to replace the royal bureaucracy and establish a United Asian Soviet Republic under the dictatorship of the proletariat. Emphasizing the "special Asian path," but retaining the vanguard of Leninism, democratic centralism, and the goal of world revolution.

Social Base: Industrial workers (heavy industry, military industry, ports), poor peasants and farm laborers, revolutionary intellectuals, and some junior officers.
Armed Forces: United Asian People's Red Army (abbreviated as "People's Red Army"), approximately 250 people.
Key Figures:

Party Chairman (Zinoviev-Mao Zedong style) – Nguyen Ai Quoc (originally named Nguyen Son, studied at Moscow Oriental University, led the General Labor Union in 1993)
Military Commander (Tukhachevsky-Lin Biao style) – Lin Bei

Territory: Controlled 80% of the inland rural areas, small and medium-sized cities, and industrial bases. Headquarters located at the "Smolny-Yan'an" Joint Command.

Slogans: "Peace, Land, Work!" "Folders of the World, Unite!"

II. Folding Trotskyism → Continuous Revolutionary Front
Self-proclaimed: Continuous Revolutionary Front/Asian Branch of the Fourth International/True Bolsheviks
He calls himself: Folding Trotskyist, Time Heretic (because they oppose the legitimacy of time folding)
Afghan Core: Believes time folding is a hoax created by the bureaucratic class and conservatives within the party, with the aim of instigating a national criminal revolution. Advocates continuous revolution, opposes "one country seizing socialism," and opposes any alliance with the bourgeoisie.

Armed Forces: Red Guard guerrillas, approximately 5-8 people, scattered underground in cities and along mountain borders.
Representative Figure: Theorist and guerrilla commander (Trotsky-Zhang Guotao style) – Plekhanov Chen (alias Chen Pojun)
Slogans: "Folding is contradiction!" "Continuous revolution until the world Soviet!"

III. Great Leap Forwardism → Great Leap Forward Commune Party
Self-Named: Asian United Great Leap Forward Commune Party / Leap Forward Faction / Commune Party
Other Names: Great Leap Forward Faction, Antiquated Steelmaking Party (derogatory)
Core of Pakistan: Achieving industrialization and agricultural socialization at the fastest speed, at any cost. Advocating "traditional steelmaking," "large-scale military operations," and militarized management.

Armed Forces: Commune Self-Defense Army (approximately 30 people, militia); Red Guard Headquarters (approximately 10 armed students)
Representative Figures:

General Secretary of the Party (Stalin-Mao Zedong style) – Jin Dayue (original name Jin Yuejin)
Red Guard Commander-in-Chief – Wen Gong (female, former university student)

Slogans: "More than fifteen years in the West!" "The Commune is paradise!"

IV. Rural-Centered Theory → Base Area Alliance (Rural Faction)

Core: Adhering to the line of "encircling the cities from the countryside and seizing politics by armed force." Emphasizing land reform as the core of the revolution, opposing premature urbanization and large-scale industrialization.

Armed Forces: The main force of the "field army" within the People's Red Army, approximately 120 people
Representative Figures:

Commander-in-Chief of the People's Red Army (Zhu De-Peng Dehuai style) – Zhu Shanhe
Chairman of the Base Area Alliance (Liu Shaoqi-Deng Xiaoping style) – Liu Nongcun

Territory: Northwest mountainous areas, North China Plain base areas, rural areas in the middle reaches of the Yangtze River. Headquarters located in "Red Flag Fort".

Slogans: "Land returns home, takes charge!" "Countries first, cities later!"

V. United Three Principles of the People → Asian United Democratic Alliance
Self-proclaimed: Asian United Democratic Alliance / Democratic League / Third Force
Afghan Core: National independence (opposition to external imperial groups), civil rights and freedoms (constitutional democracy), people's well-being (national collapse). Opposes class struggle and the dictatorship of the proletariat, but also opposes the corruption of the royal bureaucracy.

Social Base: National bourgeoisie, urban middle class, some liberal intellectuals, overseas Chinese

Representative Figures:

Chairman of the Democratic League (Sun Yat-sen-Lee Teng-hui style) – Sun Yat-sen (claims to be a descendant of Sun Yat-sen)
Military Commander – Cai Weimin

Slogans: "United nation-building, democratic unification!" "Not red slaves, not white slaves!"

VI. Imperial Asianism → Greater Asia Imperial Restoration Party
Afghan Core: Restoration of the traditional imperial order of Asia (a variant of the Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere), implementation of militarism and racial hierarchy. They believed the Asian Union's monarchy was too weak and needed more forward-looking expansion and restructuring. They were anti-communist, anti-Western, and anti-democratic.

Social base: Remnants of the old aristocracy, some warlord officers, right-wing students, and organized crime groups.

Representative figures:

President of the Imperial Asia Party (a Tojo Hideki-Yuan Shikai type) – Yuan Tiangang

Military commander – Itagaki Sakae

Territory: The northeastern frontier of the Asian Union (Manchuria/Northern Korea) and several corresponding island fortresses. Headquarters was located in "Fengtian City".

VII. Special Economic Zone Socialism → Special Economic Zone United Government

Afghanistan core: Using technocratic and market economic methods to bail out the Asian Union, preserving the revolutionary spirit in name only, but with a damaged mindset. Supporting "national planning" – managing the economy using computers and biotechnology.

Social Foundation: Technocrats, engineers, foreign-invested enterprise managers, comprador capitalists

Representative Figures:

Chief Executive of the Special Administrative Region (Deng Xiaoping-Gorbachev style) – Deng Xiaoping
Chief Engineer of the National Brain Project – Qian Zhineng

Slogans: "Development is the hard truth!" "The National Brain is the future!"

VIII. Royal Bureaucratic Group → Asian United Provisional Government

Afghanistan Core: Constitutional monarchy + bureaucratic collapse. The Emperor is retained as a symbolic head of state, but actual power is held by the bureaucratic comprador group. Support for "community modernization."

Armed Forces: "Asian United Defense Forces" (i.e., White Army/Government Army), approximately 40 personnel. Well-equipped (with Western refugees), but morale is low and internal corruption is severe.

Representative Figures:

Emperor (Nicholas II-Pu Ritual) – Hirohito Lee (Henry Lee, a figurehead emperor)
Provisional Government President (Chiang Kai-shek-Kerensky style) – Chiang Ching-kuo (strongman leader)
Commander-in-Chief of the National Defense Forces (Denikin style) – Bai Zhenguo
