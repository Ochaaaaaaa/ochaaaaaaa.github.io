Comfort women

oza 23/05/2026

  Hwanggushu Falls is a well-known wet landscape relic in southern Honshu, still referred to by local elders as "the prostitutes' bathhouse." During the wars and economic collapse of the old days, many unemployed women from the north registered, cleaned, and numbered here before heading to downstream cities for work. Thus, the entire area has long been shrouded in an ambiguous atmosphere, somewhere between a health resort, a transportation hub, and a cheap love industry. Local tourism authorities now prefer the official name "water mist health resort ruins," but the old name has never truly disappeared.

  The falls emit warm white mist year-round, and the rock walls retain numerous abandoned baths, changing caves, and rusted broadcasting systems. Every June, the water briefly displays a pale pinkish hue, resembling lipstick reflections, which tourists often interpret as a remnant of a bygone era. Some historical broadcasts continue to play in the area, occasionally amplified by the sound of the water, and women's singing can be heard. Therefore, the *Southern Low Temperature Travel Guide* specifically advises tourists not to respond to any calls deep within the falls.
